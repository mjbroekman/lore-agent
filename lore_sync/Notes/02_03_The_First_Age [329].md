Ai Xiang looked at her children and their creation, Linaria, and smiled. To create a Material Plane was no small feat. Only a few other deities had managed the miracle. Krynn. Faerun. Athas. Oerth. Eberron. Linaria. There were many demi-planes as well, but to create a full Material Plane? That was a true rarity. But her happiness was tempered with annoyance as she noticed Yuan Xiang’s footprint bubbling and frothing.

Almost all of the other gods were flitting around the world, exploring and discovering their creation. Weilang stood above the world and looked around. Slowly, she rotated her shoulders and, with a grunt, opened the cover of her tome and began to write. Words spilled across the page, filling it with everything that she saw.

Meanwhile, Rou, Zhaoman, Changguo, and Zhiyu walked the land as Eu-fen floated above. Carefully, Rou carved valleys, raised hills and mountains, dug the depths. As they did that, Zhaoman and his siblings shaped the winds and weather and life.

Changguo delighted in great claps of thunder and spinning the winds into storms around them. Zhiyu laughed lightly as she coaxed gentle rain from clouds, forming rivers to fill Rou’s carvings. Along with the rain, she brought plants from the ground and shaped the first people. Zhaoman clapped his hands at her creations and reshaped himself to their scale, laughing among them as they stared wide-eyed at the storms and rain and light of Eu-fen. He gathered them in groups and taught them of living with each other.

 

Fenfang saw this and grinned. She too reduced herself to the scale of these people and taught them language and law. Yanching joined her and taught them to protect themselves from larger creatures. And Weilang turned a page and continued to write.

Shi Chuan looked at all of it and shook his head. Once again, he picked up his walking stick and paced the land. Every now and then, he would sit down to rest and when he rose again, the land pulsed with a new force.

Chao Shang too saw the land and took to traveling it. However, he chose not to walk, but to fly. And, every so often, he would dip his flight down and tease the peoples below.

Maethirion stood with Ai Xiang and looked the rest. “They are wasting their time” he said. “These things they make? They will not last like we do.”

Ai Xiang nodded. “That is true, my child. And yet, there is delight in the creation of things that will not last. Can you fully appreciate a thing if you know it will last forever? Or is the shortness of life a test to see if you can learn a thing before it is gone?”

Maethirion scowled. “How can you fully appreciate something if you don’t have eternity to examine it?”

Ai Xiang smiled. “But if it there for all eternity, can you not take some other time to examine it? There is no need to examine it ‘now’ and so it will always be put off. But if it will perish, there is urgency.”

Maethirion frowned deeper. “I don’t feel any urgency. My creations will last forever.”

Ai Xiang raised an eyebrow. “Indeed? Your treasured Chaos will always be the same?”

Maethirion nodded. “Indeed. It will always be the Chaos for all eternity. Perfect.”

Ai Xiang chuckled. “My child, the Chaos is always changing. For all eternity. What it was before is not what it will be in the future. Even now, the Chaos shifts and changes and you will never know all of what it could be.”

Maethirion looked sharply at her and frowned. He opened his mouth to say something, but then snapped his mouth shut. He glanced around and then swore. “This is ridiculous. The Chaos just IS. Who cares if it changes? It is perfect and will last forever.”

Ai Xiang shrugged. “And yet, you will never know everything this is to know about what the Chaos was or what it will be… because you choose to believe it will always be here in its current form.”

Maethirion looked at her again and opened his mouth again, but Ai Xiang interrupted. “Oh, look at THAT. Yuan Xiang made quite an impression on Linaria. Hmmm.” He looked where Yuan Xiang had stamped their foot. It was no longer bubbling or frothing. Instead, it was plain of gray sand and dust. Zhiyu stood at the edge of it and, no matter what she tried, her coaxing of life ended where the plain began. When she brought water, it drained away from the plain. When her creatures stepped onto the plain, they weakened and died. Not even her tears brought life to the plain. Maethirion quickly descended to stand next to his sister.

“What is the problem, sister?” he asked. “Did you not know your creations would perish?”

Zhiyu looked up at him. “I knew they would, but not in such pain; or so quickly. They should have lived, but something about this plain does not want life.”

Maethirion looked at the corpses laying on the plain and walked to them. As his foot touched the gray plain, he swore and picked it up. The bottom of his foot was bleeding from numerous punctures and slices. With a word, he healed himself and then wrapped his feet in magic and resolutely walked to the corpses. He crouched next to them and contemplated their state. Lost in thought, his wings drooped and brushed the ground.

Suddenly pain lanced through his wings. His back arched suddenly and he was forced to drop his knees to the ground as well. A scream of agony pierced the sky as they touched the sand and the magic wrapped on his feet vanished. As sudden as his scream started, it cut off and he fell backwards onto the sand.

Zhiyu screamed as well and, as quickly as she could, leapt next to Maethirion and dragged him off of the plain before she collapsed as well.

Zhaoman and Rou, having heard the screams, raced to them, surrounded by some of the people she had created. Carefully, they pulled the two gods apart and laid them down. Rou examined Maethirion while Zhaoman took care of his sister.

With a cold efficiency, Rou closed most of Maethirion’s wounds and set about restoring his lost essences.

In contrast, Zhaoman carefully bathed Zhiyu’s injured hands and feet, showing the people how to care for the injuries. They gathered moss and vines and constructed a soft bed to lay her in.

Maethirion recovered first and struggled to his feet. “My wings!” he exclaimed. They were torn and bloody. Rou shrugged. “I did what I could, brother. But the damage was beyond what I could fix.”

As Maethirion spun to face Rou, his wings flared out and spray of blood flew from the feathers. Most of the blood landed nearby, in the living land, but some landed on the gray sands as well. Where the blood pooled on the living lands, great winged beasts uncurled from the ground. Dragons. Where it merely splattered on the living lands, all kinds of creatures climbed to life. Fey. Elves. Where the blood landed on the gray sand, monstrosities boiled from ground.

Maethirion looked at these new creatures and backed away. Rou faced the horrors rising from the ground and swung their hammer once. It hit the ground and, as he pulled the hammer back, mountains rose, sealing them off from the gray sands. As the mountains rose, some dragons rode the peaks as they grew while other dragons fled. At the same time, some of the fey fled into the mountains while others cowered behind Zhiyu’s bed.

As the mountains stopped growing, Zhiyu stirred. She looked around at the new creatures and smiled. “Oh you poor things. Born of fear and pain. Come, I have friends for you to meet.” She rose and beckoned them to follow. They clustered around her as she walked gingerly away from the mountains.

Zhaoman smiled and laughed as he followed, telling them of family and friendship and healing. Rou followed stoically … and Weilang turned a page and continued to write.
