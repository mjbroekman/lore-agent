# Introduction

Welcome, travelers, to Zhiedphis, a city of rich magicks, high society, and grand architecture! For sixty-three millennia, mages and master craftsmen have built this city of soaring, magic-infused buildings and defended them with weapons of fantastical power. For sixty-three millennia, the people have lived in luxury and surplus. Or so the sages say…

But we are getting ahead of ourselves or, perhaps, remembering days gone by…

# Background

Magic suffuses the very air, creating breezes to cool on hot days, heating stone floors on cold nights, creating light to chase the shadows away. Magic is as common as breathing for the residents of Zhiedphis.

Named for the mountain on which it lays, Zhiedphis is a sprawling metropolis. From the snowy peak of the eldest library spire, one can look out and see the city flow down the mountain like a blanket, sprawling across the foothills, and down to the plains far below.

Also called the Library Mountain, Zhiedphis is renowned for its scholars, sages, and, most of all, its collection of scrolls and tomes. Covering over 200 square kilometers, the city boasts more than a quarter of that in libraries alone with over 400 sq km of bookshelves across them and close to a billion tomes and scrolls.

But it was not always so. Millennia ago, Zhiedphis was but a single tower, constructed by a scholar craftsman, at the top of this lone mountain, to hold their knowledge. Though their name has been lost to the ages, the library remained and grew. Adventuring parties came and sold their relics, wizards and clerics came and studied, and soon the collection expanded beyond the lone tower into a town. This town grew into a city as tradesmen came to build more library towers and along with them came merchants and soldiers. In less than two hundred years, Zhiedphis grew from that lone tower into a fortress city that projected power through sword and scholarship. Soon it gained the reputation of being the center of knowledge in all of Linaria and, though war occasionally ravaged the lands around it, Zhiedphis remained untouched. And still, it continued to grow.

Common knowledge around the city claims that it has stood for sixty-three millennia, unmolested by monsters, gods, or demons as a beacon of knowledge. But then, many times, common knowledge is just what people want to hear…
