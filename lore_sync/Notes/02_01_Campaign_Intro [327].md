# Session 0
Today is an exciting day. You are graduating from the academy to take your place among those that scour the land for knowledge and relics to bring power to Zhiedphis! This is a joyous day in the city as the elite gather to see your graduating class off.

As you gather in the main hall with the dozens of other graduating students, as well as the next three years of underclassmen, administrators and teachers gather on the dais at the back of the hall, smiling and nodding to all. When everyone has taken their seats, the headmaster raises his hands for silence…

“You, my friends, were some of the lucky ones. Raised within the walls of Zhiedphis, the Library Mountain, you were brought up in the traditions of magic and craftsmanship melded as one.  Identified at an early age, you have all trained in your chosen path to venture forth and protect the routes between the cities. And now it is time… time for your graduation ceremony and for you to  take your places in the among the Tome Wardens of Zhiedphis.

Behold as the gods themselves welcome you into this order…”

[Various celestial and demonic beings materialize in the main hall]

The administrators drone on about responsibility and glory and the powerful mandate given to you to claim knowledge, relics, and magic from those that would misuse it. 

An hour into the ceremony, there is a loud boom and the entire hall shakes.

A look of mild consternation crosses the face of the deities present and they glare at the demons, who look confused and simply shrug.

A moment later, a robed and hooded person runs out from a back door and up to the dais. There is a whispered conversation that draws the attention of a glowing, wizened woman and a fire-wreathed young man on the dais. As they approach the robed person, the color drains from their aspects and the headmaster holds up his arms as whispers run through the crowd.

“Please! Everyone! It appears there is a minor emergency. I would ask that all graduates return to their rooms to gather their gear and then return here. Everyone else, please head to your assigned shelter and await further instructions.”

There is a moment of stunned silence as a never-before-heard wail pierces the air and everyone scatters, pushing towards the exit.

[If the players hesitate, an administrator yells at them to hurry up and go get their gear and return.]

# The Halls
As you near your shared suite, make a Wisdom save.

[Failure: You stop suddenly and are immediately run into, knocked to the ground, and trampled by other panicking students. Take 1d6 bludgeoning damage]

Outside, you see great rifts appearing in the ground, through buildings, and even in the air. The rifts remain stationary and buildings do NOT topple, but you see people fall into them.

Darkness fills some of the rifts. Searing light, flames, or ice fill others.

[Question
What do the players do:
	- Stay in room? Leave the academy? Campaign ends… they fall into a rift to the negative material plane and they die, drained of their life force.
	- Return to the hall: continue narrative..
]

You make your way back to the main hall, weaving through the crowded halls. On entering the hall, you notice that there are multiple large shimmering portals erected around the hall with lines of students in front of each one. They are being given a small sack and sent through in groups of four to six students. One of the administrators nearby notices you and calls you over.

“Come quickly! These portals should take you to safety. The rest of the city is evacuating as well!”

As you approach, he throws a sack into your arms and shoves you through the portal.

You are surrounded by light and soft buzzing sound echoes in your ears. You feel a soft caress over your skin.

After what seems like an eternity, the light fades and silence fills your ears. A voice reaches your ears:

[Each player hears a different line
	- “Find the way”
	- “Be the key”
	- “Turn the orb”
	- “Forge the path”
	- “Return the crystal”
	- “Save us all”
]

Your eyes and ears clear and you find yourself in the middle of a grassy plain. Smoke curls up into the sky off to the north.

[Casters that rely on divine or extraplanar sources, like clerics and warlocks, must make a DC 18 check against their spell casting ability.
Failure: You feel an emptiness where your connection to your patron/deity/source once was.
Success: You feel a tenuous, fragile connection to your patron/deity/source where it was once strong and vibrant.]
